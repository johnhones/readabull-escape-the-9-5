Marketing Strategy
Step 1: Price the Kindle Correctly and Publish a Paperback

At £0.77, the Kindle is priced as a launch teaser, not a commercial product. The ceiling competitor’s Kindle edition sells at £10.99 and earns £219/month from ebooks alone. The hardcover earns £3,367/month. Publishing a paperback edition is the single highest-impact format change available to you.

✓
Publish a paperback through KDP Print
A paperback cover is the ad in Amazon Sponsored Products. Physical books convert at a higher rate than Kindle thumbnails and buyers in the business-improvement space expect to pay £12–£16 for a paperback. KDP Print setup is straightforward and the Kindle edition stays live alongside it on the same listing page.
✓
Consider raising the Kindle price once reviews are established
At 0 reviews, a low Kindle price encourages trial. At 15–25 reviews, raise it to £4.99–£7.99. At that price with good reviews, conversion improves significantly and royalties increase. The ceiling title is priced at £10.99 ebook and earns 20 sales a month.
✓
Use both formats in Amazon Ads once reviews are live
Run Sponsored Products on the paperback. Paperback covers dominate search results visually and carry more authority than a plain Kindle listing. Bidding on “freedom based business book” and “escape the 9 to 5 book” with a physical product will outperform a Kindle-only listing.
↓
Step 2: Build to 15 Reviews Before Running Ads

Zero reviews is the most urgent issue. Organic visibility is already there. The missing piece is social proof. A buyer landing on your page for the first time, with no reviews and a £0.77 price, reads uncertainty. Fifteen genuine reviews transforms the perception of the title and makes ad spend work.

✓
Run an ARC campaign through BookSirens or Booksprout
Both platforms connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. BookSirens charges per download and Booksprout has a free tier. The business-improvement and personal-development readership on these platforms is large. A focused campaign could generate 15–30 reviews within 4–6 weeks.
✓
Add a review request inside the book
Place a warm, natural ask at the mid-point and at the back of the book. Readers who enjoyed it simply need a prompt. Do not ask for a specific star rating. A genuine request for an honest review is Amazon-compliant and effective.
↓
Step 3: Optimise Your KDP Keywords for Buyer Intent

Being on page one for “freedom based business” is excellent. The keyword metadata is clearly working. But Amazon gives you 7 keyword fields with up to 50 characters each. All 7 should be working to reach different buyer-intent searches in this niche : not just variations of the same term.

✓
Target the “escape the 9 to 5” keyword variations specifically
The book title is the keyword. Variations like “escape 9 to 5 book,” “quit your job book,” and “how to escape the rat race” are buyer-intent searches with real volume. These are the searches made by people who have already decided to make a change : exactly your reader.
